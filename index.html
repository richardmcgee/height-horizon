Methodology & Limitations

CDC 2000 Growth Charts

This tool uses the CDC 2000 Growth Charts, which are based on data from national health surveys in the United States. The charts describe the distribution of stature (height) by age and sex for children and adolescents aged 2–20 years.

LMS Method

The CDC charts use the LMS method (Lambda-Mu-Sigma), where three parameters describe the distribution of height at each age: L (Box-Cox power), M (median), and S (coefficient of variation). These allow us to convert any height measurement to a Z-score (standard deviation score), and vice versa.

Growth Projection

For each child, we calculate their height-for-age Z-score at each measurement point. The average Z-score represents their "growth channel." We then project forward along this channel to estimate height at future ages, up to age 20 (adult height).

Mid-Parental Height

When parents' heights are provided, we calculate the mid-parental target height:

Boys: (mother + father + 13 cm) / 2

Girls: (mother + father − 13 cm) / 2

The target range is ±8.5 cm (approximately 68% of children will fall within this range).

Important Limitations

These predictions are estimates only and should not replace professional medical advice. Growth is influenced by many factors including genetics, nutrition, health conditions, and puberty timing. The CDC data is based on US populations and may not perfectly apply to other populations. The assumption that a child will continue along the same growth percentile is a simplification — growth spurts and other factors can shift percentile rankings. Always consult a pediatrician for growth concerns.
